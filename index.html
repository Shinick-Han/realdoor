Ready, not eligible.

This service reports document readiness only. It does not decide eligibility, and nothing on this screen means approved, denied, or ineligible. A qualified housing professional makes that determination.

Get your housing documents ready for a person to review

RealDoor reads the documents for one household, shows you where on the page every value came from, and tells you what is still missing or out of date. It does not decide whether anyone qualifies for anything — a housing professional does that.

Before you start

This walkthrough takes about ten minutes. The documents for the household you choose above are already loaded, so there is nothing to upload and nothing is sent anywhere. You can stop at any step and your work stays on this device.

What happens, in order

    Step 1 of 6

    Check the values we read from your documents

    Each value below is shown together with the box on the page it was read from. Choose a field name to light up its box. Nothing here is inferred about the person.

    Step 2 of 6

    Correct a value we read wrong

    Change a value the system read, and watch the numbers underneath it move — or watch the system explain why your correction was not used.

    Step 3 of 6

    Ask what a housing rule says

    Every answer carries its rule id, the authority behind it, the date it took effect, and where in the source it is written.

    Step 4 of 6

    See how your yearly income figure was worked out

    Inputs, formula, result, threshold, comparison, effective date. A comparison is not a determination.

    Step 5 of 6

    See what is missing or out of date

    What is present, what is missing, what has expired, and what could not be dated — with the one thing you can do about each.

    Step 6 of 6

    Check what we found, then take your packet

    This is everything the earlier steps produced, in one place. Change anything that is wrong before you download it.

    This page is for anyone checking the service rather than using it. Every control below can be triggered here and its actual response shown — a disclaimer is not a control. Underneath are our own measurements, printed as they came out.

    Where the challenge's six acceptance steps live

    The challenge brief specifies a six-step acceptance demo. Our numbered walkthrough is written for the renter, not for that list, so the two do not line up one to one. This is the mapping.

    • 1. Upload documents, show extraction evidence — walkthrough step 1. The documents are pre-loaded rather than uploaded; every value carries the box on the page it came from.
    • 2. Edit one field, show downstream values update — walkthrough step 2.
    • 3. Ask a rule question, show an authoritative citation — walkthrough step 3.
    • 4. Deterministic calculation with effective dates — walkthrough step 4.
    • 5. Identify missing or expired items, export a packet — walkthrough steps 5 and 6. We split it because reviewing what is open and taking the packet are two different decisions for the renter.
    • 6. Run the refusal, prompt-injection and session-deletion tests — this page, immediately below. It is not numbered in the walkthrough because it is not a task the renter performs. Both the adversarial suite and the static no-decision guard exercise these paths on every run.

    About this build

    No external fonts, scripts, images, or analytics. Every request this page makes goes to the address in the bar above it and nowhere else; opened as a file, it makes none at all. That is a promise about this page, not about the whole system: when the question classifier is switched on, the server sends your question sentence to a model provider so it can pick a topic label. What we can promise structurally is narrower and harder: no document, no extracted field and no household record is ever part of that request. Before your sentence is sent we replace the identifier shapes we can recognise, such as an email address, a phone number or a street address. We cannot recognise all of them, so a name you type is sent as you typed it. The data source is named at the top of the page, and you can force the offline one by adding ?fixtures to the URL.
