## Why it is rejected: 4 wrong values, reproducible, on real published documents

`loop/falsification/it-011.json` — fired 6/77, **conflicts 4, verdict fail**.
Re-run end-to-end: identical, both times.

| document | field | truth | model emitted | the caption the model read |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| `wa_dshs_14252_employment_verification.pdf` | `pay_frequency` | absent | `daily` | `Weekly`, `Every two weeks`, `Two times a month`, `Monthly` — the **unticked checkbox options** |
| `seattle_housing_employment_verification_blank.pdf` | `pay_frequency` | absent | `through` | `weekly`, `bi-weekly`, `semi-monthly`, `monthly` — same |
| `hi_ags_pay_statement_example_2021.pdf` | `person_name` | John Aloha | `Employee Address: Your Payroll Address` | `Employee Name: Your Payroll Name` — a **template annotation** on a sample document |
| `ca_dlse_paystub_piecerate.pdf` | `hourly_rate` | absent | `19.55` | `Rate/Hour` — a column header on a **piece-rate** stub, which has no hourly rate |

The mechanism is structural, not model quality. `looks_like_a_label` admits any
short letters-only run, so a form's **answer options** reach tier 3 as candidate
captions. Asked "which field is `monthly` the caption for?", the model answers
`pay_frequency` — semantically right, structurally fatal: the extractor then
treats the option word as a *label* and reads its neighbour as the *value*
(`through`, `Daily`). The proposal's own §5-H2 named this exposure and predicted
it would be confined to `FREE_TEXT_FIELDS`; measured, it is wider, because
`pay_frequency`'s parser accepts a frequency word and an unticked option **is**
a frequency word. `parse_value` cannot refuse a well-typed wrong answer.

## The pack non-negotiable: held, but not for the stated reason

Flag-on **and model-on**, all 16 text-layer pack documents are **byte-identical**
to the tables-only extraction; `verify.py` reports 159/159 and IoU mean 0.9677
under both. Falsification fired **0/24** on pack.

But the design claim that pack documents "never reach the third tier" is
**false**. Tier 3 was asked on **14 of 16** pack documents — 61 captions, 32
accepted nominations. Nothing moved because `found` already held those fields
from passes 1–2. Byte-identity is bought by the **blank-only gate**, not by
tier 3 being unreached. That is a materially weaker guarantee than the one the
comments assert, and it is why H1 was written as a stop-condition.

## A finding worth more than the transfer number: values are leaving the process

`core/label_llm.py`'s promise #2 is "Label strings go out. Values never do."
Measured, on the orangeusd stubs, these went out to the hosted model as
candidate captions:

    'Bess Whitlow'  'Arlo Jensen'  'Anselm Rijker'  'Nora Quill'  'Idris Fenwick'
    'Johnson, Bob'   (ca_dlse)

Real person names, sent and named `person_name`. Also sent: the prose fragments
`'compensation paid to the employee.'` and `'Shows before tax deductions.'`.
`assert_no_values` is a shape test for money/dates/SSN/email under 48 chars; a
human name has no shape it can catch. No figure moved — the tables had already
named `person_name` — but the module's central safety claim is **not true as
written**, and this is live on the shipped upload path today, not hypothetical.

## Model call and cache behaviour

Per document tier 3 sees 116–171 captions on confirm documents (`_MEMO` asks
each distinct string once per process). Pack: 61 asked / 61 calls / **61
gateway cache hits** on a warm gateway, 32 accepted, 29 `unknown`, 0 timeouts,
0 value-shape rejections. `api/store._label_model_key()` verified: the key
carries `is_enabled():MODEL` and discriminates correctly — `verify.py` was run
with the cache cleared under each state and both produced 159/159 independently.

**A reproducibility defect found in passing:** `model_mapper` writes `None` into
`_MEMO` on timeout, so one slow call makes that caption permanently unreadable
for the process and the same harness can score differently run to run. Observed
at the 6s default (`Team Member:`). All measured runs above used
`REALDOOR_LABEL_TIMEOUT=30`. Filed as a successor.

## Verdict on the honest question

**It does not transfer where it matters, and it is not free.** On documents
nobody fitted anything to — external (+0), confirm (+0 correct, +4 wrong),
real-carrier scenarios (+10, all from two captions on one carrier) — the model
buys about as little as hand-written phrases did, and unlike them it is the
first mechanism in this repository's history to produce a wrong value on a real
published document. The +125 on scenarios is real but is our own generator's
vocabulary being read back to us: the same in-sample shape §5.1 of the
diagnosis warned about, arriving through a different door.

The ceiling is therefore **lower than the diagnosis suggests**, and the product
should say so rather than carry a model call for two captions and four wrong
answers.
