## The license: printed total closed by an arithmetic identity
Nothing new is trusted. osu net is the accepted band rule's own tail (deductions + net
= the accepted gross, one aligned column); the change only stops the multiplicative
identity — which every number beside a printed 1 satisfies — from vetoing the band.
ou hours is a factor of a row product inside an anchored run whose hours column sums
to the printed 80.00; the words REGULAR and HOURS are anchor-only strings, exact-match
after normalize_label (no fuzzy matching of OCR text), and never become fields.
Certainty stays "low"; every note names the identity and the OCR provenance.

## The hazard, named
A wrong row named REGULAR (S3 still refuses two named survivors — pinned), a qualified
hours header (lcc's `Hours or Units` refuses by its own wording, matching its
expect_absent — pinned), and the 111.75 band trap (a real earnings run keeps its
exclusion: its products are non-degenerate — pinned). Residual, carried: a true
$1.00/hr rate row on an OCR page would lose its band exclusion; the run must still sum
exactly to the accepted gross and survive S3, and no such page exists in the 77.
