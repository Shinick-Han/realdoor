## The license: physical bound
A watermark overlays a body; the two coexist by definition. A size classification that
deletes every char of a page leaves nothing for the supposed watermark to overlay, and
the page states the refuting fact itself: the minimum of its printed char sizes (27.3pt)
is at or above the threshold. WATERMARK_MIN_SIZE stays 20.0. No constant was added,
moved, or fitted.

## The hazard, named
Admitting a real watermark's glyphs into the body. Refused because the rule fires only
when the filter would remove *everything* — on any page printing the big-over-small
contrast the filter was built for, something survives and the rule never engages.
