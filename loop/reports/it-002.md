## The license: printed header row
The page declares baseline 781.0 a row of column names (four caption cells — the exact
structure `_header_row_words` already recognises, until now only to REFUSE those cells as
values), and declares column membership by span: `Johnson, Bob`[227–414] shares x-extent
with `EMPLOYEE`[228–397] and no other cell; `18.00`[989–1065] with `PAY RATE`[988–1140]
alone. No new strings (the cell must already be vocabulary), no new constants
(`HEADER_SEARCH_BAND`, `MIN_HEADER_ROW_CELLS`, the caption tests — reused, not refitted).
Refused: scaling VALUE_Y_WINDOW by page/font size (a fitted constant replacing a fitted
constant, relaxing geometry for every label everywhere); a cross-table arithmetic close for
hourly_rate (real license, but reaches only the typed field). person_name needed no
NO_SAFE_RULE: its guards (caption refusal, unique attribution, one-candidate-or-nothing,
and a shape test that may refuse the sole survivor but never eliminate a rival) held on
all 77.

## The hazard, named
A data matrix read as an identity field — osu p2's `Employee` header over five deduction
amounts, the family that once shipped person_name="Employer". Refused three ways, measured:
the decimal-aligned amounts overhang the cell and attribute to zero cells; orangeusd's
`Employee ID`, which IS attributed under `Employee Name`, dies on the caption refusal built
after those wrong names shipped; and any second candidate refuses on count. The quieter
hazard — this rule runs before page-level table_cell_value and could preempt it — fires
nowhere in the corpus: every engagement outside the two flipped docs yields zero candidates.

## Diagnosis correction, recorded
T8's it-001 root-cause note ("header path does not recognise its cells at that typography")
named the wrong mechanism. The vocabulary path recognises the cells regardless of
typography; what failed was reach — the data row is 39pt below a header and
VALUE_Y_WINDOW tops out at 22pt, and nothing else in the chain looks below a label.
backlog.json now carries the corrected note.
