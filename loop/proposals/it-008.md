# it-008 — T15 — a period-dependent rate synonym binds only when the page settles the period

## 1. Target

Backlog **T15** (opened this iteration from the filled-forms measurement), corpus
`filled` (dev split of `testdata/filled/`), doc `orangeusd_sample_paystub_filled.pdf`,
field `hourly_rate` — the corpus's **first measured WRONG value**: truth lists
`hourly_rate` in `expect_absent` (the page's `Rate of Pay` 4,812.00 is a MONTHLY
certificated salary, equal to the printed gross), and the extractor emits 4812.0 at
certainty low. The filled corpus sits outside `loop/baseline.json.field_state` (the
manifest is the 77); the flip is measured by `scripts/measure_filled_forms.py`, whose
dev headline must go from `12.5% / 1 WRONG` to `12.5% / 0 WRONG`.

## 2. The printed evidence (measured in-process on the filled PDFs)

orangeusd filled, page 1: header row `Description | Rate of Pay | Hours/Units |
Earnings …` with the single data row `REGULAR EARNINGS | 4,812.00 | 21.00 | 4,812.00`
beneath it. `Rate of Pay` normalizes to `RATE OF PAY`, which `LABEL_SYNONYMS["pay_stub"]`
maps to `hourly_rate`; `_resolve_value` finds 4,812.00 in the label's column (note:
"9 candidate value runs … 1 aligned") and emits it. Nothing on the page prints an hourly
period for that rate, and no printed `rate × hours = gross` closes over it
(swept every numeric-token pair on the page: **zero** pairs `h, g` with
`4812.00 × h = g`, `h > 0`, `h ≠ 1`). The vocabulary comment already refuses bare `RATE`
as context-dependent; `RATE OF PAY` is exactly as context-dependent — on salaried stubs
it names a salary — and was admitted anyway. The wrong value is the vocabulary's own
stated failure mode arriving on schedule.

The one document in the 77 whose correct field rides this synonym on a pay stub:
`uploads::up_016_pay_stub_wording_total_earnings.pdf` prints
`HOURS WORKED 72 | RATE OF PAY 19.25 | TOTAL EARNINGS 1386.00` — and
**19.25 × 72 = 1386.00, cent-exact, all three factors printed on the page**.

The seattle fill settles the menu question (measured, text layer):
`'Current Wages/Salary: $ 19.75 (circle one) bi-weekly semi-monthly yearly other'` with
`'hourly weekly monthly'` wrapped 4.7pt below — the drawn ellipse around "hourly" is ink
the text layer does not carry, so ALL period words appear equally. A printed menu of
periods settles nothing and must not license anything.

## 3. The license (exactly one of the five): **printed total closed by an arithmetic identity**

`RATE OF PAY` may bind to `hourly_rate` only when the page itself proves the period:
the bound value `v` closes `v × h = g` cent-exact for some printed hours-shaped token
`h` (0 < h ≤ 744, the physical bound `core.verified.FALLBACK_HOURS_BOUND` restated the
way `core.columns.MAX_PERIOD_HOURS` restates it; `h ≠ 1`, the multiplicative identity
testifies to nothing — the it-004 `_degenerate` precedent) and some printed cents-form
money token `g`, both on the value's own page, neither being the value's own run. A rate
that closes the product with same-page hours and gross has proven its own period; a rate
that closes nothing has an unstated period, and the binding is refused → abstention.

Weighed and REFUSED — printed periodicity adjacency (an "hourly"/"per hour" marker on
the label's own line): the only wage-adjacent "hourly" anywhere in the 77 + filled dev
is the seattle MENU above, where the marker license's firing shape is exactly the
false-fire shape (all menu words equal in the text layer; a wrap could strand "hourly"
on the label's line with its rivals on the next). Zero validating instances, one
measured hazard shape → not shipped. If a future page prints an honest marker we
abstain, which is the bias this loop wants.

Weighed and REFUSED — demoting the synonym outright: `up_016::hourly_rate` is measured
correct through it and G3 forbids flipping a correct field. Demotion remains the
fallback if falsification finds the licensed version firing wrongly anywhere.

Scope: `pay_stub` only. The `employment_letter` table carries the same string and the
same conceptual hazard, but `up_018` and `ho_006` are measured-correct through it with
no closable identity on their pages (employment letters print no gross), so licensing
it would be two G3 regressions; no wrong has been measured there. Recorded as a hazard
below and in the report as a residual, not patched by enlarging this change.

## 4. The rule (one seam, inside `core/extract.py`)

A frozen table `PERIOD_DEPENDENT_LABELS = {"pay_stub": {"RATE OF PAY": "hourly_rate"}}`
and one intercept in `_scan_page`, after the whole value-rule chain and immediately
before `found[field_name] = resolved`: when the resolving label normalizes to a
period-dependent entry for this document type and `resolved` carries a value (not an
abstention), `_rate_period_closure(lines, resolved)` must find the closing pair or
`resolved` is replaced by `_abstain(...)` with a note naming the refusal. A licensed
binding's emission is byte-identical to today's (no note added). Flag:
**`REALDOOR_RATE_PERIOD_LICENSE`** — on by default; `=0` skips the intercept and
restores bit-identical behaviour. No other function changes; the exact-vocabulary pass
cannot hit the table (LABEL_MAP has no `RATE OF PAY`), and the recorded abstention
blocks the model-mapper stage by the existing `field_name in found` rule.

## 5. The hazards, named (each with the refusal that carries it)

- **The anchoring twin, `HOURS/UNITS`** (filled value 21.00 is working DAYS): must stay
  OUT of every vocabulary. Pinned twice: a test asserts `HOURS/UNITS` is a key of no
  label table, and the orangeusd end-to-end test asserts `regular_hours` stays abstained
  and 21.00 is emitted for no field. Do not fix a context-dependent label by admitting
  its context-dependent neighbour.
- **A coincidental closure licensing a salary**: `v × h = g` closing by accident. The
  guards: cent-exact equality, `h ≠ 1` (else `rate = gross`, `hours 1` — the salaried
  stub's own shape — would self-license), `h ≤ 744`, `g` in cents form, `h`/`g` not the
  value's own run, `v > 0`. Measured on the target: zero closing pairs among all 45
  numeric tokens.
- **The menu** (seattle): no marker license is shipped at all, so a menu can license
  nothing. Pinned by a seattle-shaped synthetic test and by the seattle end-to-end
  state (all-abstain) staying put.
- **A refused binding blocking a later, honest label**: a page printing both
  `RATE OF PAY` (refused) and, lower, `HOURLY WAGE` would keep the abstention by
  first-occurrence-wins. Refusal-biased (abstention, never a wrong value); no such page
  exists in the corpus (the sweep is the record).
- **Re-entry after the abstention**: `table_cell_value` may try the abstained field at
  page level; on the target its row-name test refuses (`REGULAR EARNINGS` ≠ `REGULAR`).
  The two-run sweep of the full extractor is the proof nothing re-emits anywhere.
- **The employment_letter twin** (scope boundary): `up_018`/`ho_006` stay correct and
  unlicensed by construction; pinned end-to-end. The residual is recorded, not hidden.
