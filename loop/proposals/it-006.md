# it-006 — T12 — the single-row column: a printed total of one member, and what 96 dpi actually leaves standing

## 1. Target

Backlog **T12** (design it-D of `ocr-extension-design.md`), corpus `confirm`,
`lcc_understanding_your_paycheck.pdf`: gross_pay (truth `$325.81`) and net_pay
(truth `$264.45`) — both `image_only`, on the 96–116 dpi document the design ranked
highest-risk ("attempt once, accept NO_SAFE_RULE readily"; backlog expected_yield 0,
best case 2).

## 2. The printed evidence (measured this iteration at NATIVE scale — which is not the dossier's)

The dossier OCR'd lcc at render scale 3.0; the injection layer reads each region at
its own native resolution, and at 96–116 dpi the evidence FRAGMENTS across pages
(`loop/worktmp/ocr_design/probe_t12.py`):

* **p1** (the whole stub at 96 dpi): the summary band's rows survive ($61.36,
  $264.45, $113.20) but the hours token `31.00` and the summary's current gross are
  NOT detected — no product, no anchor, where the band lives.
* **p3** (crops at ~111 dpi): the earnings row reads whole —
  `31.00 x $10.510000 = $325.81` exact — and the single-row column prints its own
  total: `Total: $325.81`, x-aligned beneath the row amount (right edges 0.7pt
  apart). The band's members are not in these crops (no 264.45 anywhere on p3).
* **p2** (summary strip at ~117 dpi): the band `61.36 + 264.45 = 325.81` closes —
  and the strip also prints two live misreads: `$113.20` reads `'5113.20'` (a `$`
  that became a digit and PARSES as money) and the YTD amount reads `'$4,209.35'`
  where p1 reads `'$4,209.38'` — the document's digit flips in the flesh.

So no single page co-locates an anchor with the band: gross can close on p3 by the
page's own printing; net cannot close on any page. The honest reach is **+1**, not
the design's best-case 2, and the reason is measured, not assumed.

## 3. The license (exactly one of the five): **printed total closed by an arithmetic identity**

A single-current-period-row column still prints a total; what it cannot give is a
multi-member sum, so the `_nonzero >= 2` guard rightly refuses it. What licenses a
one-member run: the row product `rate x hours = amount` (both factors printed whole,
neither the multiplicative identity, hours within the physical bound, factors and
amount non-overlapping) closing on an amount that the page REPRINTS as another
cents-form token on a different line, x-aligned with it — the column's own printed
total, the design's "second printing corroboration" made structural. Two independent
tokens must carry the same cent-exact value AND a printed product must close over
one of them; a digit flip in either member breaks the pair (measured on this very
document: the two printings of the YTD amount differ, `'…35'` vs `'…38'`, and are
refused by exactly this equality). Everything downstream — S3 one-distinct-survivor,
V1/V2/V3, the band search for net, certainty "low" — is the committed chain,
untouched. Scope: OCR-injected pages only, flag **`REALDOOR_OCR_SINGLE_ROW`** (on by
default; `=0` ⇒ byte-identical output).

## 4. The rule (one mechanism, inside `core/verified.py`)

`verify_page` gains `single_row: bool = False` (passed by `extract_document` as
`bool(injected) and _ocr_single_row_enabled()`). With it on, exactly one thing
changes: after `_anchored_runs`, the anchored list is extended by
`_single_row_runs(tokens, bound)` — one-member pseudo-runs built as section 3
states (product non-degenerate per it-004's `_degenerate`, hours ≤ bound,
`_overlaps` checks as in `_anchored_runs`, amount and reprint both `decimals == 2`,
reprint on a different baseline of the same page, x-aligned on either edge, both
factor orientations deduplicated). The pseudo-run's total is the reprint token;
its line joins the band-exclusion set like any earnings row. Gross candidates,
the net band search, hours/rate factor columns (which need ≥ 2 rows and therefore
never speak for a single row — lcc's `expect_absent` demands exactly that), S3 and
the vetoes all run unchanged.

## 5. The hazards, named (each with the refusal that was measured or pinned)

- **The document's own digit flips** (`'5113.20'`, `'$4,209.35'` vs `'…38'`,
  Banner ID `X00123458`, `'Sap 15, 2017'`): `5113.20` parses as money but no product
  closes over it and no second printing matches it; the YTD pair differs across
  printings and is refused by cent-exact equality; the date and ID are not
  emittable by any identity path (`VERIFIABLE_FIELDS` is the numeric four) — all
  pinned as must-stay-abstained tests on the real document.
- **The YTD single row** (`$4,209.3x` + YTD total `$4,398.36`): not equal, no
  product closes on it — no pseudo-run, no second gross survivor (S3 would refuse
  two). Measured: the only pseudo-run on all probed documents is lcc p3's.
- **A same-line twin masquerading as a reprint** (osu prints Current = YTD side by
  side): the reprint must sit on a DIFFERENT baseline; same-line twins are refused
  (measured: osu forms zero pseudo-runs — its `x 1` products are also degenerate).
- **expect_absent rate/hours** (truth lists both): `_factor_columns` requires ≥ 2
  anchored rows to tell the factor columns apart, so a single row can never emit
  either — structural, and pinned end-to-end.
- **net through a coincidental band**: on p3 nothing sums to 325.81 (measured,
  bands = []); net stays abstained on every page, and the report records that as
  the honest per-page limit, not a residue to patch.
- **`Total Employer Contributions` 113.20 adjacency**: never a candidate — no
  product closes over it and it reprints nowhere x-aligned.
